Step 01

Kharat: The Rebirth of Cloth

Before a single drop of pigment touches the canvas, the cloth must be birthed. Kharat is the ritual of transformation where raw, hand-woven Khadi is prepared to hold the weight of divine legends.

Bhilwara • Canvas Preparation

The Sacred Kalap

A starch made of boiled wheat flour and Gond (local resin) is manually rubbed into the fibers. This "Kalap" makes the cotton non-porous, ensuring that the mineral colors sit majestically atop the surface rather than soaking through.

Maida • Natural Resin • Starching

The Mohra Finish

The final touch is the Ghonta. Using a smooth agate stone called a Mohra, the artist burnishes the dried cloth until it acquires the texture of ivory and a subtle, celestial sheen.

Agate Stone • Burnishing • Final Polish
